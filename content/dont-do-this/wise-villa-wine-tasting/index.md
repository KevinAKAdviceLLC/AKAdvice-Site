## The Visit

We took a couple of potential clients up to [Wise Villa Winery](https://wisevillawinery.com/) in Lincoln for an afternoon tasting, figuring good wine and no agenda would do more for the relationship than another slideshow ever could. We were right. The estate sits up on a hill in the Sierra foothills with rows of vines running out toward the horizon, and we grabbed a table on the patio with a flight of five pours each.

Turned out our clients are young parents too, with an infant just a few months old — and once that came out, the "getting to know you" small talk turned into a genuine conversation that ran a lot deeper than we expected.

### The Conversation We Didn't See Coming

Somewhere between the Albariño and the Cabernet Franc, one of them laughed and said, "Sorry, we probably smell like a diaper bag." We told them ours smelled the same way for the better part of a year. That cracked the door open.

They talked about the 2am feedings, the way nobody warns you how loud a quiet house gets once the baby finally sleeps, and the strange guilt of wanting twenty minutes alone with a glass of wine. We swapped notes — what worked, what didn't, the gear that was worth it and the gear that's now collecting dust in a closet. At one point Dad said, "Nobody tells you the hard part isn't the baby, it's remembering you're still a person," and the table went quiet for a second before everyone just nodded.

By the last pour, we weren't really talking business anymore, and that was fine. Some of the best client relationships start exactly like that — two families comparing notes on the same exhausting, wonderful year, with good wine as an excuse to sit still long enough to say it out loud.

## Lessons Learned / Tips

- A relaxed tasting room beats a conference room for actually getting to know a client — no clock, no agenda, no pressure.
- Wise Villa's patio is a great spot for a longer sit-down conversation; go on a weekend afternoon and give yourselves time to linger.
- If you find out mid-conversation that you have more in common with a client than business, let the conversation go there. It's usually worth more than the pitch.
